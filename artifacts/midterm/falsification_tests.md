Falsification Tests
User testing
1. Falsification of Universal Utility (The Complexity Threshold)
We assumed our cognitive scaffold lowers activation energy for all tasks.
Evidence (User 6): "I think it is a good idea but I would stop using it after a couple of times because I would forget about it or just take the two minutes on my own instead of putting it into the app."
System Insight: Our app cannot be a universal task manager. For tasks that do not induce cognitive paralysis, the act of opening the app and inputting the goal can add friction. The system only provides leverage when a task is complex or unfamiliar enough to require a scaffold.
2. Falsification of the Medium (The Environmental Trap)
We assumed a digital tool could successfully coach initiation without interference from the host device.
Evidence (User 1): Doesn't know if she would use it, since if her phone is telling her to do stuff she magically ends up on Instagram.
System Insight: Our scaffold does not operate in a vacuum. For a user with low executive bandwidth, the smartphone itself acts as a dopamine trap, depleting their limited cognitive resources before initiation can occur. This hard constraint validates our technical decision to enforce a strictly black-and-white, minimalist UI to avoid contributing to cognitive overload.
3. Falsification of the Bottleneck (The Working Memory Prerequisite)
Our core hypothesis assumes the user remembers the goal but cannot initiate it due to cognitive paralysis.
Evidence (User 3): "I usually forget what my goals are about a week or two in; I want my goals to change into a habit because I am not doing it every single day. It has to be an alarm but I fall back into my routine"
System Insight: This testing proved that for state-depleted users, working memory often fails before initiation friction even begins. If the goal drops out of working memory, our breakdown scaffold is irrelevant. Our app should not solve the problem of working memory, but is also dependent upon the user having a goal in-mind that causes user pain.
