---

## Who They Are

Marcus has made four investments in productivity software. None of them are in his own rotation. He evaluates tools at a level of abstraction that keeps him permanently above the actual experience of using one.

He has strong opinions about the consumer mental health and neurodiversity market — done the research, knows the incumbents, can quote the TAM. What he doesn't bring up in investor meetings is that he sold his second company eighteen months ago and has not, since then, been able to start working on anything. He tells himself this is intentional. He calls it a "strategic pause." His wife calls it retirement. He calls it thinking.

There is a half-finished book. Three project ideas that have never made it past a Notion page. A woodworking bench he assembled and then never used. He is not, it turns out, a person who is naturally good at unstructured time. He was always good at the work because the work had deadlines and stakeholders and someone expecting something. None of his personal projects have that.

He downloaded MicroDeck out of professional curiosity. He has not fully admitted to himself that it's also personal.

**His inner monologue at first launch:** *"No login. Good call — removes a conversion barrier. Minimal onboarding. Brave, but they're betting the core mechanic can carry it. 'What feels big and difficult right now?' Hm. That's more direct than I expected. More honest."*

---

## Professional / Behavioral Profile

- **Primary mode:** Pattern-matching to known products; evaluates through the lens of market positioning before personal experience
- **Blind spot:** Difficulty engaging with a product as a user rather than as a potential asset; personal stakes are present but suppressed
- **Emotional signature:** Controlled and measured; uses investor language as a buffer; more candid in post-session conversation than during active use
- **App habits:** Notion for everything, then abandons it; recently cycled through Roam, Bear, and Obsidian in a three-week spiral that produced nothing

---

## Relationship to MicroDeck

- Will evaluate the "no accounts, no sync, no backend" decision through a scalability lens — and may initially miss why it's a feature
- The 2-minute timer framing will register as a product bet; he'll have an opinion on whether it's the right bet
- Personal use case exists but won't be volunteered; post-session questions are where it surfaces
- Most valuable output is his reaction to what MicroDeck *doesn't* do, and whether that reads as principled or underdeveloped
- May be the most surprised by his own reaction if the app works on him

---

## Key Questions This Persona Answers

- Is the product's positioning legible to someone who knows the market deeply?
- Does "no gamification, no analytics, no backend" read as a principled stance or a gap in thinking?
- Will the personal use case emerge despite the professional frame?
