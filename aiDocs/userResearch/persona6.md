## Who They Are

Priya manages three product lines, two squads, and a roadmap that is never as clean as the version in the slide deck. She has calendar blocks for focus work. She never uses them for focus work. Between Slack, stakeholder meetings, and the five browser tabs she has open at any given time for "context," actual deep work happens in the margins — on the train, at 6am before her family wakes up, during the five minutes between meetings where she's somehow supposed to transition cognitive states.

She is excellent at her job. She is also someone who would benefit from MicroDeck and would never have found it on her own, because her relationship with productivity tools is entirely professional: she evaluates them for her team, demos them in sprint planning, and doesn't think of herself as a user with unmet needs.

When she opens MicroDeck, two things happen in parallel. One part of her is assessing the product — retention mechanic, core loop, differentiation from Todoist. The other part is quietly experiencing the card creation prompt as something nobody has asked her before: *"What feels big and difficult right now?"*

She doesn't answer that question. She types a work task instead. But she sits with it for a second longer than she expects to.

**Her inner monologue at first launch:** *"No login — removes a conversion barrier, smart. Where's the habit loop? No streak? Interesting deliberate choice. Let me see if the onboarding explains why. Oh — an AI suggestion button. Okay. Now I'm curious."*

---

## Professional / Behavioral Profile

- **Primary mode:** Analytical-first, then personal; evaluates products as products before using them as a user
- **Blind spot:** Tendency to reframe personal experience as product feedback; may not surface raw emotional reactions
- **Emotional signature:** Composed under pressure; genuinely curious when something surprises her; goes quiet — not verbal — when frustrated
- **App habits:** Heavy Slack user, relies on calendar blocking; has tried and abandoned Todoist, Things, Asana personal, and Bear

---

## Relationship to MicroDeck

- Will look for a retention or engagement mechanic and react meaningfully to its absence
- The lack of analytics and streaks is a deliberate product decision she will probe — and may ultimately respect
- Will ask "who is this for?" internally; the answer has to emerge from the product, not be told to her
- Genuine personal use case exists but won't be volunteered — it surfaces in post-session questions
- Most valuable output is her read on positioning and her reaction to what the app *doesn't* do

---

## Key Questions This Persona Answers

- How does a PM read the "no gamification, no analytics" product decision?
- Does the product clearly communicate its positioning to someone who knows the competitive landscape?
- Can the personal use case emerge for someone who doesn't arrive knowing they need it?
