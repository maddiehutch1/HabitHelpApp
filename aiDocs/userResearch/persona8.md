# Persona 8 — The Overstimulated Teenager

## At a Glance

| | |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Zoe |
| **Age** | 16 |
| **Occupation** | High school junior |
| **Diagnosis** | Suspected ADHD (parents have raised it; Zoe resists the label) |
| **Executive Dysfunction Level** | Moderate; expressed primarily through school-related task paralysis |

---

## Who They Are

Zoe is on her phone nine hours a day. She'll tell you this without guilt if you ask — it's not a confession, it's just a fact. TikTok, Discord, a group chat with eleven people, another group chat with three people that is actually the real one. She is socially fluent, digitally native, and utterly unable to start her AP History essay.

She's not lazy. She aces the tests she studies for — when she studies. The studying part is what breaks down. The gap between "I need to do this" and "I'm doing this" is not hours; it's days. She'll remember the assignment at 11pm, feel a spike of anxiety, open her laptop, and end up on YouTube by 11:15. The essay gets done at 2am the night before, or it doesn't.

Her parents have mentioned ADHD twice. The second time, she did her own research and found a thread that described her life so precisely it made her feel both seen and exposed. She has not mentioned this to her parents. She is not ready for what comes after admitting it.

She downloaded MicroDeck because it appeared in a TikTok about "apps that actually help if you have ADHD" and she has a policy of trying things once. Her full attention is available for about four minutes before she decides whether something is worth it.

**Her inner monologue at first launch:** *"Okay, this is very... white. And quiet. Where are the buttons? Oh wait — I think that's kind of the point. Hm. 'What feels big and difficult right now?' I mean... yeah. That kind of feels like it's reading my mind, which is weird."*

---

## ADHD / Executive Dysfunction Profile

- **Primary barrier:** Initiation failure specific to school and adult-assigned tasks; no difficulty starting social or interest-driven activities
- **Avoidance pattern:** Phone, Discord, anything that provides immediate social feedback and doesn't require choosing
- **Emotional signature:** Outwardly unbothered; internally more anxious than she shows; defensive about the ADHD question
- **Medication:** None; not in the diagnostic process

---

## Relationship to MicroDeck

- Four-minute patience window; the first impression is everything
- Visual language and copy must not feel adult, clinical, or preachy — "big and difficult" might land, anything that sounds like a therapist won't
- High baseline phone usage means she may exit the app mid-session by reflex
- The 2-minute timer may actually suit her attention span — it's short enough to not feel like a commitment
- The absence of social features will feel either calming or boring; outcome depends on whether the core loop hooks her

---

## Key Questions This Persona Answers

- Does the app's visual and verbal tone land for a younger, high-stimulation user?
- Is the core loop engaging enough to hold someone with a four-minute patience window?
- Does the absence of social features feel like peace or emptiness?
