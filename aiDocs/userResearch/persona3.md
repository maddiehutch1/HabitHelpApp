## Who They Are

Dani has alarms for everything. 7:00am: wake up. 7:10am: actually get up. 7:20am: leave for shower. Not because they're forgetful — because every transition requires a prompt, and without prompts, time disappears. Their camera roll is full of photos of things they need to remember: a sticky note, a label on a shelf, the instructions for something they need to do later. This is the system.

On a hard day — and hard days are not the exception — getting out of bed, brushing teeth, and making coffee are each a separate impossible task. Not laziness. Not choice. The gap between wanting to do something and doing it is enormous and has always been enormous. Dani has made peace with that being structural, not moral. The people around them have not always made the same peace.

They work because they have to, and because the physical, predictable rhythm of warehouse work suits them better than knowledge work ever could. But burnout has reduced their hours, and the unstructured time at home is worse than being busy.

They don't read app descriptions. They opened MicroDeck because someone on an ADHD subreddit said it helped them during freeze mode. That was enough.

**Their inner monologue at first launch:** *"Okay. Just... just show me what to do. Please don't make me read anything."*

---

## ADHD / Executive Dysfunction Profile

- **Primary barrier:** Complete task sequencing failure; can't determine the first step of anything, including familiar tasks
- **Avoidance pattern:** Not avoidance — genuine inability to initiate; freeze mode is the baseline, not the exception
- **Emotional signature:** Deep fatigue; shame exists but is muted by exhaustion; relief when something works is disproportionately intense
- **Medication:** Vyvanse (prescribed), access inconsistent due to national shortage and cost
- **Coping strategies (active):** Voice memos, alarms, asking others to walk them through tasks verbally
- **Coping strategies (failed):** Written to-do lists, calendars, anything requiring daily setup or maintenance

---

## Relationship to MicroDeck

- Will likely try the voice FAB before typing anything
- Fresh Start must be immediately readable; any appearance of data loss will cause genuine panic
- The full-screen, minimal timer is well-suited — UI noise is a real barrier
- Will not read help text or tooltips; navigation must work by instinct
- "What feels big and difficult right now?" may deeply resonate or trigger shutdown depending on the day
- Most likely to return if the app helped them actually start something, even once
