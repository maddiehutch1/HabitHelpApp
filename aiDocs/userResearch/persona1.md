# Persona 1 — The High-Functioning Avoider

## At a Glance

| | |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Jordan |
| **Age** | 31 |
| **Occupation** | UX Designer at a mid-size tech company |
| **Diagnosis** | ADHD-Combined type (diagnosed at 26, medicated inconsistently) |
| **Executive Dysfunction Level** | Mild to moderate |

---

## Who They Are

Jordan's apartment is spotless. Their Notion workspace has 47 pages, color-coded by project, with nested databases and linked templates for tasks they've never started. They have a morning routine they follow perfectly — until they open their laptop and spend the next two hours in a planning loop that produces nothing.

They are genuinely good at their job. Reviews say they're thoughtful, creative, and sharp in critique. What the reviews don't capture is the two hours spent rearranging browser tabs before writing a single line of that report. Or the evening they spent building a new task management template instead of doing the task.

Jordan talks about their ADHD with fluency — they've read the books, follow the ADHD creators on YouTube, and can explain dopamine dysregulation to anyone who asks. The knowledge doesn't help. If anything, it's become another system to optimize.

They approach MicroDeck the way they approach every new app: with a detailed mental checklist and one foot already out the door.

**Their inner monologue at first launch:** *"Okay. Clean design. Good sign. Let me check settings first. Wait — there's almost nothing in settings. Is that a feature or is this unfinished? Let me read the onboarding copy more carefully."*

---

## ADHD / Executive Dysfunction Profile

- **Primary barrier:** Task initiation despite complete awareness of the task
- **Avoidance pattern:** Over-planning and tool-switching as a substitute for doing
- **Emotional signature:** Mild shame about the gap between capability and output; frames avoidance as "refining the system"
- **Medication:** Adderall, taken irregularly — skips on weekends, forgets on bad days, doesn't fill the prescription on time
- **Coping strategies (active):** Body doubling, Pomodoro, time-blocking, working from cafes
- **Coping strategies (failed):** Every major productivity app at some point; the graveyard is long

---

## Relationship to MicroDeck

- Will spend time in Settings before creating a first card; sparse Settings will either impress or frustrate them
- Genuinely curious about the AI suggestion feature — will test it critically, not just casually
- The "two minutes" framing will land only if it feels like a design choice, not an arbitrary default
- Most likely to return if card creation is faster than opening Notion — the bar is low but the comparison is real
- At risk of dismissing the app as "too simple" if the onboarding doesn't communicate its intent

---

## Key Questions This Persona Answers

- Does minimal feel intentional or unfinished to a power user?
- Is the value proposition legible without explanation?
- Does the AI suggestion feature feel genuinely useful or performative?
