# Persona 4 — The Burnout Professional

## At a Glance

| | |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Sam |
| **Age** | 41 |
| **Occupation** | Senior project manager at a consulting firm |
| **Diagnosis** | None; no clinical history |
| **Executive Dysfunction Level** | Low baseline; moderate under stress |

---

## Who They Are

Sam has a reputation. Reliable. Thorough. The person who sends the agenda before the meeting and the summary after. In twelve years at the firm, they have never missed a deadline. They have also, for the past eighteen months, been running on something between fumes and spite.

A demanding client engagement, a parent's health decline, and a home renovation that will never end have stacked in ways Sam didn't see coming. They are now the kind of person who stares at an unopened email for forty minutes. Who moves things from one part of their to-do list to another without doing them. Who describes the sensation as "my brain has a blocker I didn't approve."

They are not seeking a diagnosis. They resist the idea that what's happening to them has a clinical name — partly because it feels like an excuse, and partly because they've watched the diagnostic process swallow two colleagues into a medicalization spiral they didn't want. They want to get through the next week.

Sam has zero patience for apps that waste their time. They allocate ten minutes to evaluate any new tool; if it doesn't deliver something useful in that window, it's deleted. They resent onboarding. They resent tooltips. They will respect a product that treats them like an intelligent adult and have no patience for one that doesn't.

**Their inner monologue at first launch:** *"Okay. Two minutes. Fine. If this is just a Pomodoro with extra steps I'm going to be annoyed. But let's see."*

---

## ADHD / Executive Dysfunction Profile

- **Primary barrier:** Situational initiation failure from mental fatigue and cognitive overload — not a neurological difference, a resource depletion
- **Avoidance pattern:** Pseudo-productivity — meetings, file reorganization, easy tasks that create the feeling of progress
- **Emotional signature:** Frustration at underperforming relative to their own standard; no shame spiral, but strong aversion to feeling "broken"
- **Medication:** None; manages with exercise, sleep hygiene, and periodic therapy
- **Coping strategies (active):** Calendar blocking, working from coffee shops, colleague check-ins
- **Coping strategies (failed):** Time-tracking apps (felt punishing), journaling (felt performative)

---

## Relationship to MicroDeck

- Will read onboarding copy critically — cliché or vague language will land badly
- Has no frame for "habit initiation app"; the value must be self-evident in the first session
- Least likely of all personas to use voice input
- The 2-minute timer may feel too short or patronizing without context
- Most likely to return only if the first session produced a tangible result — they actually started something

## Key Questions This Persona Answers

- Does MicroDeck's framing alienate users who don't identify with ADHD or dysfunction labels?
- Is the 2-minute timer legible and non-condescending to a high-functioning user in a temporary slump?
- Can a first-time user complete the core loop without external context?
