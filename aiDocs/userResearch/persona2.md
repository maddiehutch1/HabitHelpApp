## Who They Are

Mara's phone has 23 apps in a folder called "maybe this one." She downloads them on good days, when motivation feels like a personality trait she possesses. By the third day, the guilt of not opening them is heavier than whatever problem they were supposed to solve. She stopped checking that folder six months ago.

She works in bursts — two focused hours, then nothing for three days. When she's in the burst, she's extraordinary. When she's not, she's on her couch at 11pm watching someone else organize their studio on YouTube, thinking *I should be doing that.*

Mara manages her ADHD without medication because the one stimulant she tried at 24 made her anxiety unbearable. She's been in therapy for two years. She knows the patterns. She still can't break them. Therapy has made her a better narrator of her own suffering without making it easier to start a commission.

Her friends describe her as warm, funny, and unreliable. She would describe herself the same way, but the unreliable part isn't funny from the inside.

She downloaded MicroDeck because a friend mentioned it in passing. She didn't look it up first. She just opened it, which is its own kind of miracle.

**Her inner monologue at first launch:** *"Okay. This is... actually calm. Wait, what am I supposed to write here? 'What feels big and difficult right now?' Everything. All of it. I don't know how to answer that."*

---

## ADHD / Executive Dysfunction Profile

- **Primary barrier:** Decision paralysis and task overwhelm; can't identify a starting point when everything feels urgent
- **Avoidance pattern:** Passive scrolling, busywork, watching other people be productive
- **Emotional signature:** Anxiety about not doing compounds the inability to do; shame spirals after bad days
- **Medication:** None by choice; manages with therapy and caffeine
- **Coping strategies (active):** Writing things down on paper, texting a friend for accountability
- **Coping strategies (failed):** Reminder apps, habit trackers, journaling apps, the folder on her phone

---

## Relationship to MicroDeck

- The opening prompt "What feels big and difficult right now?" may feel too open — she might freeze at the blank field
- The "I'm stuck" and "Make this smaller" buttons are potentially the most valuable feature for her specifically
- Will not explore Settings; needs the core loop to earn her trust before she investigates anything else
- Visual calm matters — any notification, badge, or streak indicator would be an immediate turnoff
- Most likely to return if the app made her feel *relieved* rather than accomplished

---

## Key Questions This Persona Answers

- Is the card creation copy emotionally safe for someone prone to shame spirals?
- Does the "I'm stuck" AI flow reduce load or create a new decision point?
- Is the first-launch experience welcoming enough for someone who expects to fail again?
