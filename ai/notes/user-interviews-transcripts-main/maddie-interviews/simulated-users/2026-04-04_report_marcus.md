### Settings

**First impression:** Two settings. I exhale slightly. No tracking dashboard. No engagement metrics. No streak display.

**Evaluative notes:** From an investor perspective, my first reaction is: this product has no retention mechanic. No streak means no FOMO. No history means no sunk-cost effect. No scoring means no competitive motivation. The conventional wisdom on consumer apps says you need at least one of these.

My second reaction: this is a deliberate anti-engagement-optimization stance. The product is betting that users who need it will return because they need it, not because the app manufactured a reason.

I think about this for a moment. It's principled. It may be right for this specific problem. Habit-initiation for executive dysfunction users may require a different retention model than generic habit apps — shame-based retention mechanics (streaks) actively harm the target user. The product has apparently understood this.

**Copy and tone:** "AI Suggestions — Sends text to OpenAI." — Correct disclosure. Rare.

**Emotional response:** Respect for the discipline of the settings screen. It would have been easy to add a streak tracker. They didn't.

### Just One Mode

**First impression:** One card, centered. [Start] and [Not today].

**Evaluative notes:** This feature solves the deck-growth problem elegantly. The product's core promise is "one card, two minutes" — but the deck view with multiple cards slightly violates that promise. Just One Mode restores it. Good product thinking.

## Overall Assessment

**Strongest moments for this persona:**
- Timer Screen (Running) — the constraint worked on a user who believed himself too sophisticated to be constrained; this is the most meaningful validation of the core mechanic
- Archive Prompt — produced a genuine emotional response in a persona whose primary mode is emotional suppression; "rest it" landed accurately
- Settings screen — the deliberate absence of retention mechanics earned respect from someone who would have initially flagged the absence as a weakness

**Biggest friction points:**
- [⊟] Just One Mode icon: non-standard, opaque, important — discoverability needs improvement
- "Tiny" used three times across the creation flow: mildly condescending register for a high-status professional user, even when the mechanism is working
- F8 proximity: Marcus would potentially bypass the app on days when the avoidance is moderate rather than severe; the app's conditional utility needs clearer positioning in onboarding

**Likelihood to return:** High (conditional)
Will return when book avoidance recurs — which it will. Return is driven by need rather than manufactured engagement, which is actually the right retention model for this product and this user.

**The one change that would most improve this persona's experience:**
Add a single line to the Welcome Screen copy that sets a clearer expectation of when to use the app: "For the moments when knowing isn't enough to make you start." — this preemptively frames the product as a specific tool rather than a general task manager, reducing the F8 misalignment risk for professional users who might otherwise reach for it in the wrong context.
