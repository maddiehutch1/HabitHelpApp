## Screen-by-Screen Review

**Copy and tone:** "Start the thing you keep putting off." Okay that's kind of uncomfortably accurate. Like, I just thought about the essay and then I read this. Weird.

"One card. Two minutes. That's it." — I read this and my brain does that thing where I immediately suspect it's going to be more complicated than it says.

**First impression verdict:** I'm not bouncing out immediately. Which is more than I expected from a TikTok recommendation. The stillness is different. I'm used to apps that try to grab me. This one isn't trying. That makes me curious.

**Interactive affordances:** [Let's begin →]. The arrow makes me feel like something is about to happen. I tap it.

### Timer Screen — Running

**First impression:** Full screen. No bottom nav. Just my task and the countdown and a dot.

**The pulsing dot:** I watch it for a few seconds. It's moving slowly. Like breathing. I think "okay that's kind of a lot" and then I stop thinking about it and read the essay prompt on my laptop. I come back to the timer. I'm on minute 1:00. I've already re-read the prompt and made a mental note about the main argument. That took less time than I thought.

**Cognitive load:** Zero. There's nothing to do. I notice my reflex to pick up my phone — the phone I'm already holding — but there's nowhere to go from this screen. That's actually helpful.

**Friction points:** The notification from Discord that came through while I was in the timer was the actual threat. I unlocked the phone to check it (by reflex) and the timer was still running. That's the environmental trap, not the app.

**Delights:** The fact that the screen stays on so I don't have to unlock it to check how much time is left.

## Overall Assessment

**Strongest moments for this persona:**
- AI suggestion chips on CreateCardActionScreen — "Read the essay prompt again" was the correct first step and Zoe recognized it immediately; the AI suggestions earned trust with this persona
- Timer Screen constraint — the reflex to check Discord was triggered but had nowhere to go; the locked-down screen worked as designed
- Just One Mode — experienced as the fulfillment of the welcome screen's promise; "this is the actual version of the app"

**Biggest friction points:**
- VoiceAISuggestionsScreen → serial creation flow: described as "kind of long after the easy voice part"; the tone discontinuity between effortless voice input and multi-screen creation is particularly jarring for a high-stimulation user with a four-minute patience window
- [⊟] icon: completely opaque; Zoe tapped it accidentally and liked what she found, but accidental discovery is not a reliable interaction design pattern
- Fresh Start copy: initial alarm at "blank deck" recurs for this persona; the second sentence resolves it but the framing order works against calm reading

**Likelihood to return:** Medium-High
Four-minute patience window means return depends on whether the app is findable on a cluttered home screen and whether the first session established enough value to compete with Discord and TikTok. First session did establish value. Return is plausible but not guaranteed.

**The one change that would most improve this persona's experience:**
After VoiceAISuggestionsScreen [Add selected], replace the per-card serial creation flow with a batch-add confirmation — show all selected tasks as pre-created cards and add them to the deck in one tap — eliminating the multi-screen overhead that breaks momentum after successful voice capture.
