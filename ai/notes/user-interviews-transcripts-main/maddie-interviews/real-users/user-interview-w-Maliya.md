# ⭐ Debrief

## Most Useful Features

* Timer
* AI suggestions

  > “Oh this is how small my task can be”

---

## Least Useful / Ignored

* None — participant indicated they would likely use all features

---

## Timer Feedback

* Prefers **simple numeric timer (calm)**
* Ring/progress visuals may:

  * Increase stress for some users
* Suggested:

  * “Extra time” feature (timer continues counting after completion)

---

## Task Behavior Preference

* Strong preference for **single-task focus**
* Notes:

  * Others may prefer switching between tasks
  * Switching can help avoid burnout for some users

---

## Workflow Fit

**What would make this fit better into your routine?**

* Home screen **widget/reminder**
* Daily planning integration:

  * Add tasks in the morning
  * Use throughout the day

---

## Product Framing

**How would you describe this to a friend?**

> “It gives you a start. It gets you motivated to get going no matter how big or small the task is, in a very small and clear way.”

---

## Overall Feedback

* **4 / 5 recommendation**

* No major usability issues identified

---

# ⭐ Key Observation

> Participant interpreted the app more like a **to-do list (task completion)** rather than a **task initiation tool**

### Implication:

* Potential **positioning gap**
* Risk of feature drift toward productivity tracking vs. initiation

---

# ⭐ Key Insights (Synthesis)

### 1. Timers are a core behavioral anchor

* Not just helpful — **central to user workflow**

### 2. Desire for completion feedback

* Even minimal:

  * “Done” state
  * Small sense of accomplishment

### 3. Simplicity is working

* Calm design + low friction = strong positive signal

### 4. Feature clarity needs improvement

* Tappable elements (e.g., settings) unclear
* Missing affordances (checklist / completion)

### 5. Product positioning risk

* Users default to:

  * “task manager” mental model
* Instead of:

  * “start tool” / activation tool
