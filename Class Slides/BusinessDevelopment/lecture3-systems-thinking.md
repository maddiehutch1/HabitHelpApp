## Why This Lecture Matters

- Problems don't exist in isolation
- Most failures are systemic
- Seeing the system changes what's possible

## Rethinking Systems

> Most failures aren't because people are stupid or unethical. It's because they don't understand the systems that they're in.

- Almost no one questioned the system itself
- If you can't see the system, you can't see the space of possible actions

## What Is a System?

> "A System is an interconnected set of elements that is coherently organized in a way that achieves something."

Three components:
- **Elements**
- **Connections**
- **Goal or Purpose**

## Anatomy of a System

### Stocks & Flows

- **Stocks** — the memory of the history of changing flows in the system

### Feedback Loops

- How changes to the stock influence the flows that go back into the stock

## System Traps

- **Bounded Rationality**
- **Rule Beating**
- **Success to the Successful**
- **Tragedy of the Commons**

## Don't Blame People

- Systems shape behavior
- *"For if you suffer your people to be ill-educated, and their manners to be corrupted from their infancy, and then punish them for those crimes to which their first education disposed them, what else is to be concluded from this, but that you first make thieves and then punish them."*

## Leverage Points: Interacting with & Changing a System

### Surface-level leverage
- Parameters
- Thresholds
- Numbers

### Structural leverage
- Feedback loops
- Incentives
- Information flows

### Deep leverage
- Goals
- Rules
- Assumptions / paradigms
