# Lecture 6: Choosing the Right Customer & Differentiation

*Why most projects fail before they start*

## The Framework

- Right Customer
- Right Problem
- Right Competition
- Right Differentiation
- Right Hypothesis

## Is This a Customer?

- "Small Business Owners" — **No.**
- If you cannot name a specific human in a specific moment, you don't have a customer

### The Standard

If you can't name them, you can't test them.

## Key Findings From Customer Misalignment

1. Your pain does not equal market pain
2. Advertising & visibility do not fix weak positioning
3. Knowing who your real customer is changes the battlefield

## Step 1: The Customer

- Who exactly is the customer?
- **Be specific**
- If you can't name them, you can't test them

### Poster Board Questions:
- Who are they?
- Where do you find them?
- What context are they in when the pain hits?

## Step 2: Pain/Problem

- What are they trying to accomplish?
- What goes wrong?
- Why does it matter?

### Fill In:
- What's happening?
- What are the stakes?
- Evidence: workarounds, complaints, time spent

## Step 3: Competition and Alternatives

- Competition always exists
- Tools, workarounds, or doing nothing
- Go for the 800 LB Gorilla first

### Questions:
- What do they do today?
- Why is it "good enough"?
- Where does it fail?

## Step 4: Differentiation

- Why would they switch?
- What changes the tradeoff?

### Differentiation: Choose Your Battlefield

- "Attack where your competition is weakest"
- Differentiation is the essence of your strategy
- Good differentiation is what makes your product click with customers

### The 2x2 Battlefield

- Pick axes that matter to your customer
- Be optimistically realistic

### Questions:
- What's uniquely better?
- What's hard to copy?
- What moment makes them say "oh — this is better"?

## Falsifiability

Apply falsifiable thinking to your hypothesis.
