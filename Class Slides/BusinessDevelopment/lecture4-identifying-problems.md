# Lecture 4: Identifying High-Quality Problems

*And What To Do With Them*

## Today's Focus

- Picking problems worth solving
- Why outcomes are decided before you build
- Preparing to sprint on the most important challenge

## Our North Star

- Right customer
- Right problem
- Early validation keeps failure cheap

## Key Insight

- You can build the "right solution" to the wrong problem
- Most waste is upstream
- Better mental models lead to better problems, which lead to better outcomes

## Why Problem Quality is a Leverage Point

> In systems: local wins can create global losses. If the system collapses, nobody wins. Don't blame people — blame the system.

## Activity: Problem Pitches

- 60–90 seconds each
- Pitch the **problem**, not the solution
- No interruptions, no fixing

## Activity: Choose One Example Problem

Pick one problem to use today:
- Feels real
- Has stakes
- Seems testable
- Not your team project

## Step 1: The Customer

- Who exactly is the customer?
- **Be specific**
- If you can't name them, you can't test them

### Poster Board Questions:
- Who are they?
- Where do you find them?
- What context are they in when the pain hits?

## Step 2: Pain/Problem

- What are they trying to accomplish?
- What goes wrong?
- Why does it matter?

### Fill In:
- What's happening?
- What are the stakes?
- Evidence: workarounds, complaints, time spent

## Step 3: Competition and Alternatives

- Competition always exists
- Tools, workarounds, or doing nothing
- Go for the 800 LB Gorilla first

### Questions:
- What do they do today?
- Why is it "good enough"?
- Where does it fail?

## Step 4: Differentiation / Advantage

- Why would they switch?
- What changes the tradeoff?
- What makes it "click"?

### Questions:
- What's uniquely better?
- What's hard to copy?
- What moment makes them say "oh — this is better"?
- What are your first principles?

## Write the Founding Hypothesis

### Write: "We're Wrong If..."

## Step 6: Cheap Loops

- Cheap tests beat expensive opinions
- Always shippable, always learning
- Keep failure recoverable

### Goal: Learn fast if we're wrong

- No "full build" allowed
- Pick 1-2 tests that:
  - Involve real customers
  - Can run in days, not weeks
  - Generate disconfirming evidence

## A Warning: Cargo Cults

- Don't memorize rituals
- Understand purpose
- Tools are for learning faster
